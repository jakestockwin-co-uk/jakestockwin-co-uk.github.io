I have also tutored a number of other applied mathematics courses on the Oxford undergraduate syllabus, and co-supervised undergraduate dissertations and summer projects.

Every year, I deliver mathematical epidemiology lectures on the Wellcome Trust Mathematical Models for Infectious Disease Dynamics course in Cambridge.

In the past I have supervised part IA Mathematical Biology in Cambridge, as well as lecturing and running the associated practical component for part of the course (Matrix Modelling and Applications, Easter term). I have also held a non-stipendiary applied mathematics lectureship at Worcester College, Oxford (also where I studied when I was an undergraduate!)
